Frequently asked questions

1. How does the filter affect water wastage?

By installing a tank in, e.g. a basement or a technical room, water would not need to be sent directly to a water treatment plant. Instead you would have your own "treatment plant", which would give you access to clean water without it having to pumped into your house.

2. Do the filters have to be changed?

Yes, no water filter lasts forever. In order to maintain low cost of water filters, we would provide an exchange service, where used filters could be returned in exchange for a fresh filter, at a lower cost than simply purchasing a new filter. The used filter would then be sanitised and turned into a fresh filter, reducing the need for producing brand new filters, which in turn reduces costs and the impact on the environment.

3. How do we plan to distribute the filters in developing countries?

As the filters are light and portable, transporting filters is not difficult. A few people could take several filters with them for exchange, or a single truck could pick up used ones, while delivering fresh ones.

4. How do the filters work during droughts?

The filters can be mounted to most water tanks, making long term storage feasible. By filtering water as it is poured, you wouldn't need to worry about bacteria contaminating the water during storage. Thus people in developing countries would have access to clean water until emergency supplies can arrive.

Our mission here at waterWai is to bring clean water to every corner of the world and reduce water waste while minimizing our impact on the environment.
